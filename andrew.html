Welcome!

After graduating from the UW-Madison last May with B.S. degrees in Applied Environmental Economics and Anthropology, I currently work as a Quantitative Risk Analyst with HC Tech (formerly Henning and Carey Technologies) in Chicago, IL.

CME Trading Challenge 2016

The CME Trading Challenge is an annual trading competition in which universities around the world compete to make the highest returns in the futures markets over a two-week time period in February. I competed for UW-Madison in 2014-2015 and got a great opportunity to use software like Trading Technologies (TT) and CQG to visualize and discover various strategies in financial markets

Since I've graduated and technically can't compete anymore, I've decided to come back and guest lecture the current team via Skype each Friday. The team is led by AAE professor Sheldon Du.

Socio-Economic and Racial Disparity in the Darbo-Worthington Park Neighborhood of the City of Madison

For my undergraduate thesis, I conducted a research and community evaluation project involving U.S. Census data and community surveys focusing on an area just north of the capitol called Darbo-Worthington Park.

The research was directly related to the non-profit organization Sustain Dane's smART program, a community-based artistic involvement program for people of all ages in Madison.
